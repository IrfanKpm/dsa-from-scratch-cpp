## 🎯 Focus: Two Pointer Technique

One of the most important and frequently used problem-solving strategies in DSA is the **Two Pointer Technique**.

The Two Pointer approach helps in solving problems involving:
- Arrays
- Strings
- Linked Lists
- Searching and optimization problems

It is especially useful for reducing time complexity from **O(n²)** to **O(n)** in many cases.

---

## 📌 What is the Two Pointer Technique?

The Two Pointer technique uses two pointers (or indices) to traverse a data structure in a coordinated way.

Instead of using nested loops, we maintain two pointers that move based on certain conditions.

### Common Patterns:
- **Opposite Direction Pointers**
  - One pointer starts from the beginning, the other from the end.
  - Example: Pair sum in sorted array, palindrome check

- **Same Direction Pointers (Fast & Slow)**
  - Both pointers move in the same direction at different speeds or conditions.
  - Example: Removing duplicates, linked list cycle detection

---

## 💡 Why Two Pointers?

- Reduces time complexity significantly
- Eliminates unnecessary nested loops
- Simple and efficient for sorted or linear structures
- Widely used in coding interviews
